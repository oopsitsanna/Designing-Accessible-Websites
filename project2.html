Accessible Data Tables

Alt text refers to invisible description of images and used within an HTML code. Alternative text is very important in a way to identify images which have no text associated them. People using screen reader programs to browse the web cannot see or hear what the image represents unless a short label using the alt attributes code to describe the appearance and function of an image on a page.
Guideline 1.1 - Text Alternatives page provides general guidelines on what kinds of text to use in an Alt attributes and discusses alternate text requirements for images.

Captioning Video

Captioning video content increases accessibility, encourages better engagement, and even improves your search engine optimization. Captioning your videos can have a big effect on how successful they are. There are many benefits of it. For instance, not everyone can hear your video, or some people can located in a loud place or can't turn video sound on. Also, subtitles improve comprehension, so people are more involved in the video while reading the subtitles. Some people may not speak your language, too. Captions can growth your chance of getting noticed and remembered.
Captions (Prerecorded) by World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) regulates all important terms and processes of captions.



Links and Hypertext

To make a website accessible developers also need to use links so that people with disabilities or those who likes to use the tab key can use them approprietly. Link text requires meaningful contrast with surrounding text and there is an additional visual cue when the link receives mouse or keyboard focus.

W2C clearly identifying and describing the target of the link. Link Purpose can be intent to the link text with its determined context what is described in W3C Success Criterion 2.4.4.
